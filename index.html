What we collect

Across all of these apps, the answer is almost nothing. We want to be exact:

Your content — stays on your device

Everything you enter (pantry items, tasks, notes, custody days, pool tests, study schedules, moods, photos of receipts, and so on) is stored locally on your phone in the app's own database. We have no account system and no backend, so we never receive, see, or store it. Deleting the app, or using the in-app “Delete all my data”, removes it permanently.

Purchases — handled by Google Play and RevenueCat

If an app offers a one-time purchase to unlock it and you choose to buy, the payment is processed by Google Play Billing (or the Apple App Store on iOS) together with RevenueCat, which we use to manage purchases and let you restore them on a new device. For that purpose only, RevenueCat may process your purchase history and an anonymous purchase/device identifier. We never see or store your card number — the store handles payment. This data is used solely to deliver and restore your purchase; it is not sold and not used for advertising.

What we do NOT collect

  • No name, email, phone number, or account — the apps don't ask you to sign up.
  • No advertising and no third-party analytics or tracking SDKs.
  • No location, contacts, browsing history, or health data.

Device permissions

Some apps ask for a permission to do their job. Each is used locally on your device and nothing is uploaded:

  • Notifications Local reminders (expiry dates, test days, chores) scheduled by your phone — no server involved.
  • Calendar TermFlow and CustodyBook can write your classes or custody days into the calendar you choose, on your device, only when you turn it on.
  • Camera / Photos CustodyBook can attach a photo of a receipt to an expense; the image is stored locally with your record.

Children

ChoreJar is designed for families to use together on a parent's device; it has no accounts, no ads, and collects no personal information from anyone, including children. Our other apps are general-audience and not directed at children.

Your control

Because your content lives only on your device, you are always in control of it. You can export a copy from within most apps, and you can erase everything at any time with “Delete all my data” or by uninstalling. To manage or request a refund on a purchase, use your Google Play or Apple account.

Changes

If we add a feature that changes what data is involved — for example, turning on optional cloud backup in the future — we will update this page and the app store data disclosures before that feature ships.
